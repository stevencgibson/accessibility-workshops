a11y and the law

Equality Act 2010 (UK)

  • replaced the Disability Discrimination Act
  • does not explicitly mention the web
"a person (a “service-provider”) concerned with the provision of a service to the public or a section of the public (for payment or not) must not discriminate against a person requiring the service by not providing the person with the service."

Section 508 (US)

  • signed into law in 1998
  • covers software and hardware
  • but only applies to Federal agencies

Americans with Disabilities Act (US)

  • signed into law in 1990
  • requires employers, state and local governments to offer reasonable services or tools to ensure that people are not discriminated against on the basis of disability
  • cited in a11y cases against AOL and Target

Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG)

  • authored by W3C
  • v1.0 became a recommendation in 1999
  • v2.0 became a recommendation in 2008
  • used in US, Australian and Canadian law relating to govt. services
  • three levels of conformance - A, AA, AAA

WCAG

Level A (covers basic issues)

  • text alternatives to non-text content
  • content is coded in a logical order
  • "skip to content" links
  • no autoplay on videos, banners etc...
  • keyboard-accessible (with exceptions)
  • descriptive link text
  • and more...

WCAG

Level AA (covers critical issues)

  • audio descriptions for video
  • no images of text
  • text can be resized to 200% without loss of function
  • clear keyboard focus
  • consistent navigation
  • fore/background contrast ratio of at least 4.5:1
  • and more...

WCAG

Level AAA

  • sign language translations for video
  • text alternatives to video
  • fore/background contrast ratio of at least 7:1
  • explain abbreviations
  • keyboard-accessible (w/o exception)
  • and more...

What's the deal with alt text?

Informative images

Stirling Citroen
  <dl>
    <dt><img src="location.png" alt="Branch" /></dt>
    <dd>Stirling Citroen</dd>
  </dl>

Decorative images

  <img src="car.jpg" alt="" />

Functional images

  <a href="/print">
    <img src="print.png" alt="Printer-friendly version" />
  </a>

Images of text

    <img src="ferrell.gif" alt="I'm gonna get you, get you drunk. Get you drunk off my lady hump. My hump, my hump, my lovely lady hump." />

Complex images

    <img src="chart.jpg" alt="Android's market share has risen from 49% in 2014 to 59% in 2015, outstripping the growth of it's main competitors Windows and Mac." />

DO NOT

    <img src="car.jpg" alt="An image of..." />

Screenreaders will flag when there's an image so this will create duplicate content.
